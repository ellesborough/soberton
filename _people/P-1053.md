Born in Soberton, the tenth child of Samuel and Martha. The 1901 census lists the family at Cold Hill, with his father working as a Navvy. By 1911 his mother had died and the family lived at West Street. Some time around then he worked as a garden boy.  
 He joined the Royal Navy on his birthday (Military no. J.28342) and joined the crew of HMS Impregnable 5th Nov 1913 as a Second Class Boy before being promoted to First Class after 8 months. He then served on HMS Endymion and HMS Victory I, before being stationed on HMS Bulwark 26th August 1914.  
 He died aged16, in an internal explosion that destroyed [HMS Bulwark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Bulwark_(1899)) off Sheerness, the second worse accidental explosion in UK history; 736 men lost their lives.  
 Commemorated on Soberton War Memorial (as well as Portsmouth Naval Memorial)  
   
 His enlistment documents list him as 5'2" with Brown hair, grey eyes, and a fresh complexion.
