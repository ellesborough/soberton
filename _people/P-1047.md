Born in Soberton, the second of seven children of William and Mary Rye, baptised 5th June 1896 in East Meon.  
 1901 census lists him living at Coombe Farm, East Meon with his mother Mary and 3 brothers  
 1911 census lists him at Ingoldfield Cottages, Soberton with both his parents and his 5 younger siblings (his elder brother had died). His father was working as a Carter on the farm.  
 He joined the Royal Marine Light Infantry, RN 22nd January 1916.  
 Killed at [Battle of Arras](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Arras_(1917)) in France during WW1  
 Commemorated on Soberton War Memorial, burried in France.  
 [CWGC](https://www.cwgc.org/find-records/find-war-dead/casualty-details/1638059/robert-william-rye/) lists next of kin as "Mrs Mary Rye; Maple Cottages, Soberton"  
   
 1st RM Bn. War Diary states on 23/4/1917 "Large carrying party to Gavrelle - 2 other ranks wounded, 5 killed"
